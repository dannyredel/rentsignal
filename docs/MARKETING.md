## 1. Brand Naming

### Locked: "RentSignal" ✅

| Aspect | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Clarity | Good — "Rent" + "Signal" = clear it's about rent intelligence |
| Memorability | Medium — clean but generic, many "Rent-" prefixes exist |
| Domain | rentsignal.de ✅ available (€5.93/yr), rentsignal.app ✅ (€11.03/yr) |
| SEO | English-first, doesn't rank for German queries organically |
| BeeSignal family | Strong connection — "Signal" brand family (BeeSignal → RentSignal) |
| International | Works globally, no linguistic barrier |

### Alternative Name Options

| Name | Pros | Cons | Domain risk |
|------|------|------|-------------|
| **MietOptimal** | German-first, self-explanatory ("optimal rent"), SEO-friendly for DE queries, sounds professional/B2B | Limited to German-speaking markets, harder for expat/investor audiences | mietoptimal.de likely available |
| **MietSignal** | German + Signal brand family, clear meaning ("rent signal") | Mixing languages feels awkward in German | mietsignal.de check |
| **Mietkompass** | Strong metaphor ("rent compass"), trustworthy feel, 100% German | Doesn't scale to English markets, "Kompass" is overused in German fintech | mietkompass.de check |
| **RentLens** | Analytical feel, works in EN, implies "seeing clearly" | No German recognition, generic | rentlens.com check |
| **MietCheck** | Action-oriented, instant comprehension in German, sounds like a tool you'd use | Too narrow — sounds like a one-time check, not an intelligence platform | mietcheck.de likely taken |
| **Rentwise** | EN-friendly, implies intelligence, clean | No German signal, could be confused with financial advice | rentwise.com check |
| **MietPilot** | Navigation metaphor, German-first, implies guidance | Overused "Pilot" suffix, sounds more like an app than a platform | mietpilot.de check |
| **Preisschild** | Means "price tag" — direct, punchy, German | Too literal, doesn't imply intelligence/optimization | preisschild.de check |
| **Pretium** | Latin for "price/value", professional, institutional feel (like Predium) | Not German-recognizable, no SEO benefit | pretium.de check |
| **MietIQ** | Short, punchy, implies intelligence, German+English hybrid | Could sound gimmicky, "IQ" suffix overused in SaaS | mietiq.de check |
| **Rentalyze** | EN-friendly, implies analysis, tech feel | No German SEO, could be confused with US rental tools | rentalyze.com check |

### Decision: RentSignal (Locked)

**Why RentSignal won:**
- EU expansion-ready — works in English across all regulated markets (Austria, Netherlands, France, Sweden)
- BeeSignal brand family — "we built BeeSignal for consumer research, now RentSignal for rent intelligence"
- Conny precedent — they went FROM German (Wenigermiete.de) TO international (Conny). Don't repeat their first mistake.
- German SEO comes from content language, not brand name — "Mietpreisbremse Rechner von RentSignal" ranks fine

**Domain:** rentsignal.de (€5.93/yr, available). Buy .app (€11.03/yr) as secondary.

## 3. Channel Strategy

### The Conny Problem

Conny has massive ad spend across YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, and Google Search. **We cannot outspend them.** But we don't need to — they target tenants (B2C), we target landlords (B2B). Different audience, different channels, different message.

| **TikTok / Instagram Reels** | Heavy (tenant viral) | NOT our channel for launch. Tenants watch TikTok for rent tips. Landlords don't. Revisit Month 6. | €0 | P3 |

### The Asymmetric Strategy: Authority vs. Volume

Conny wins on **volume** — millions of ad impressions, viral TikToks, YouTube pre-rolls. They can do this because their CAC is funded by success fees (€1,000+ per converted tenant).

We win on **authority** — real data, published methodology, satellite features nobody else has, dual-method validation. Our content is cited, linked, referenced by journalists and researchers.

```
Conny's funnel:       TikTok ad → "Are you overpaying?" → Free calculator → Lawyer → Fee
Our funnel:           SEO article → Free compliance tool → "You're leaving €X on the table" → Pro trial → Subscription
```

**Key insight:** Conny's advertising actually HELPS us. Every Conny ad that makes a landlord nervous about compliance is a potential RentSignal customer. We position as the solution to the problem Conny creates.

Every Conny ad, TikTok, and YouTube video that says "Check if you're overpaying!" creates **compliance anxiety among landlords**. This is our acquisition trigger.

**Our message to landlords:**
> "Conny is telling your tenants they're overpaying. Are they right? Check for free in 10 seconds — before your tenant does."

This is the single most effective positioning: **we're the landlord's defense against Conny.**

## 5. Social Media Strategy

### LinkedIn (Primary Channel)

**Why LinkedIn:** Property managers, Hausverwaltung employees, real estate investors, PropTech builders are all here. Conny has no LinkedIn presence (their audience is consumers, not professionals).

**Content pillars:**

1. **Data insights** (2×/week) — Share a chart, a number, a finding.
   - "We analyzed 10,275 Berlin apartments. Restaurant density within 1km is the #1 rent predictor. Not location. Not size. Restaurants."
   - "Kitchen renovation: +€4.01/m² causal impact. Balcony: -€0.72/m². Don't build the balcony."
   - Map visualizations from satellite data

2. **Methodology transparency** (1×/week) — Build credibility by showing HOW.
   - "We matched 2,288 apartment pairs to isolate the causal effect of kitchen renovation on rent. Here's what we found."
   - "Why SHAP values beat correlation: the difference between 'apartments with kitchens cost more' and 'a kitchen CAUSES higher rent.'"

3. **Regulatory updates** (as needed) — Become the go-to voice.
   - New Mietspiegel publication → analysis post
   - Legal ruling on Mietpreisbremse → implications post
   - §559 reform discussions → impact analysis

**Posting schedule:** Mon/Wed/Fri, 8:00 AM CET (German professional peak)

### YouTube (Secondary, Long-form)

**Format:** Screen-share tutorials + data walk-throughs. Not polished productions — authentic, educational, expert-level.

**Series ideas:**
- "Mietrecht für Vermieter" (Rental law for landlords) — 5-10 min episodes
- "Berlin Rent Data" — Monthly market analysis with real charts
- "Die Renovierungs-Frage" (The renovation question) — Feature vs. ROI deep dives

### TikTok / Instagram (Not Yet)

Conny owns the tenant-side short-form video space. We shouldn't compete there in MVP. **Revisit in Month 6** if we want brand awareness among tenants (for the freemium funnel).

If we do enter: data visualization reels ("Most overpriced PLZ in Berlin", "Neighborhood that rose 40% in 5 years") — educational, not promotional.

**We are:**
- The calm expert in a noisy room (Conny shouts, we explain)
- Data-first, opinion-second
- Bilingual and international
- Slightly contrarian (challenge common assumptions with evidence)

**We are NOT:**
- A legal service (we don't give legal advice, we provide intelligence)

## 9. Conny Intelligence Brief

### What Conny Does Well (Respect the Competition)
- **Brand simplicity:** "Conny" is memorable, human, approachable. Changed from "Wenigermiete.de" (descriptive but clunky).
- **Viral mechanics:** Free calculator → shows emotional number ("You're overpaying €X/month") → converts to paid.
- **Multi-channel presence:** YouTube pre-rolls, TikTok reels, Instagram stories, Google Search ads — heavy, consistent, everywhere.
- **Success-based pricing:** Zero risk for tenants eliminates conversion friction.
- **Expat targeting:** English-language calculator targets Berlin's huge expat market (probably their highest-value segment given 83% contract violation rate).

### What Conny Does Poorly (Our Opportunities)
- **Calculations are structurally incorrect** — documented by German real estate law blogs. They misapply Mietspiegel range classifications.
- **Self-classification bias** — Tenants guess their apartment's equipment category. Garbage in → optimistic estimates → disappointed customers.
- **No landlord product** — 100% tenant-side. Landlords who get Conny letters have nowhere to go for help. We ARE that help.
- **No intelligence beyond compliance** — Conny tells you IF you're overpaying, not WHY, not what to do about it, not whether renovation makes sense.
- **Expensive** — 5-6× monthly savings. A €200/month reduction costs the tenant €1,000-1,400 in fees. Users complain about this.
- **No LinkedIn/B2B presence** — They don't talk to property managers or Hausverwaltungen. That entire market is open.

### Conny's Ad Spend Creates Our Demand

```
Conny runs TikTok/YouTube ads → Tenants learn about Mietpreisbremse
→ Tenants use Conny calculator → Some file complaints
→ Landlords receive Conny letters → Landlords panic
→ Landlords Google "Mietpreisbremse Vermieter" → They find US
```

**Every € Conny spends on advertising generates demand for our product.**
