Everyone else is either one-time strategic (consultants, survey tools) or continuous operational (price management platforms). BeeSignal is the only player combining *continuous* + *strategic*.

### The Core Narrative

Most SaaS companies set their pricing once — through guesswork, competitor copying, or a single research project — and then leave it untouched for 12–24 months. During that time, competitors adjust, the product ships new features, customer segments shift. But the pricing stays frozen.

The result is invisible revenue leakage. Not dramatic churn, but a slow, silent erosion: customers concentrating on the cheapest tier, upgrade rates declining, feature-tier misalignment growing.

**Traditional pricing research gives you a snapshot. BeeSignal gives you a live feed.**

## 3. What We Sell

> **Confidence that your pricing is optimal — not just today, but continuously.**

Three capabilities that no one else brings together:

1. **Experimental demand measurement** — Choice experiments on simulated demand that map the full willingness-to-pay landscape across customer segments, feature bundles, and price points. Not guessing from historical data. Not running risky live A/B tests. Validated at r=0.80 against real purchases.

2. **Continuous pricing monitoring** — Live tracking of how pricing performs against predictions, integrating billing data, product analytics, and competitive intelligence. A Pricing Health Score that tells you at a glance whether your pricing is working. Powered by DiD and event studies that isolate true revenue effect from noise.

3. **Signal-triggered recalibration** — When drift is detected, BeeSignal designs and runs a targeted re-study, seeded with real behavioral data from the monitoring layer. Each cycle makes the next prediction more accurate. The calibration loop is the moat — no competitor can replicate it without the same installed base.

| Competitor | What They Do | The Gap |

| Simon-Kucher / Conjointly | One-time strategic engagements | They walk away after delivery. BeeSignal stays. |

| Optimizely / Statsig / Eppo | A/B testing on live traffic | Test deployed changes only. Can't explore undeployed pricing space. |
| ProfitWell / Baremetrics | Descriptive MRR analytics | What happened, not what to do. |
| Paddle Pricing | Validated demand, productized as consulting | $20K–$100K+. BeeSignal is the self-serve product layer they left unoccupied. |

### vs. Each Category

**vs. Pricing consultants (Simon-Kucher, Conjointly)**
They deliver one-time engagements with expensive, slow traditional research. BeeSignal delivers faster initial research + continuous monitoring. They walk away after the engagement. BeeSignal stays.

**vs. Price management platforms (PriceFx, PROS)**
They optimize *within* existing pricing structures using historical transaction data. BeeSignal questions the structure itself using experimental demand estimation. They're pricing operations. BeeSignal is pricing intelligence. Not competitors — potentially complementary.

**vs. A/B testing tools (Optimizely, LaunchDarkly)**
They test one variable at a time on real customers — slow and risky for pricing. BeeSignal explores the full price/packaging space synthetically first, reducing the A/B testing search space from hundreds of combinations to a handful of high-probability winners.

**vs. Analytics tools (Stripe Analytics, Baremetrics)**
They show what happened (descriptive analytics). BeeSignal shows what happened *relative to what should have happened* (predictive benchmarking). Raw metrics vs. conjoint-benchmarked diagnostics.

## 7. Sales Motion

### Phase 1: Land with the Study, Expand with Monitoring

1. **Land:** Sell the demand study. This is the proof of value.
2. **Deliver:** Run the study, present recommendations with clear predictions (plan mix, conversion rates, ARPU).
3. **Bridge:** At delivery, introduce the 90-day check-in: "We've given you the optimal pricing. In 90 days, we'll check whether reality matches our predictions."
4. **Expand:** At the check-in, present continuous monitoring: "Instead of checking in every 90 days manually, we can watch your pricing continuously and alert you when something shifts."

### Phase 2+: Lead with the Loop

Once established with case studies:
- **Lead with the problem:** "Your pricing has a shelf life. Do you know when it expires?"
- **Show the loop:** Simulate → Price → Track → Recalibrate
- **Anchor on the outcome:** "Our customers maintain optimal pricing year-round, not just the week after a consulting engagement."
- **Close on risk reduction:** "What's the cost of running suboptimal pricing for 6 months without knowing it?"

## 9. Market Context

### Why the Gap Exists

- **Attention poverty:** Average SaaS company spends just 6 hours on pricing strategy *ever* — not per year, ever
- **Quality gap:** Only 6% of SaaS companies have done rigorous demand research; 48% have done nothing
- **Price barrier:** The credible solution (Paddle/ProfitWell) runs $20K–$100K+ per engagement
- **Wrong tools:** A/B testing treats price as a UI element; up to 80% of pricing A/B tests fail to generate actionable insights
- **Collective underpricing:** 37.5% anchor on competitors who are themselves anchoring on competitors — SaaS companies underprice by 30–85% after product improvements

### Why No One Owns This Category Yet

1. **Translation barrier** — methodology lives in academic econometrics; no one has built the UX layer
2. **Unnamed category** — "Continuous Pricing Intelligence" doesn't exist as a search query yet; first-mover advantage is real
3. **False data barrier** — people think SMBs lack data, but DiD/event studies only need Stripe panel data
4. **Integration assembly** — connecting Stripe + Amplitude + competitive intel is a data engineering project, not a feature toggle
